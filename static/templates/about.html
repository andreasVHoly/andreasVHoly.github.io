About


tl;dr

I am currently doing my Honours in Computer Science at the the University of Cape Town, having completed my Bachelor of Science (BSc) at UCT in Computer Science and Computer Games Development in 2015. I also tutor two third year courses for the Computer Science department as well as private clients. My honours project involves working on an Information Retrieval project intended to create a digital archive for online resources in official South African Internet-Scarce Languages, where I am doing the Web development section.


Background

I moved from Johannesburg to Cape Town in 2010 with my family, which at the time was as if the world ended for me. Coming to Cape Town was in hindsight the best move of my life, as Cape Town is such an amazing place and suited my outdoor lifestyle much more than the concrete Jungle. I attended the German school both in Johannesburg and Cape Town, before starting my Undergrad at the University of Cape Town in 2013. My school did not offer any Computer Science courses, which left me very undecided in what I wanted to do after Matric. Thankfully I had applied to UCT for Computer Science, which I in the end accepted as my career choice, not at all knowing what I was getting into. Luckily enough I picked up coding extremely fast and loved every bit of it. In my undergrad I took electives in other fields that interested me, such as Astronomy, Archeology and Film.


Honours Year so far

In my honours year I have tried to take a diverse number of courses to experience as many areas of Computer Science as possible. Of particular interest where the Networking courses, which included Network and Internetwork Security, Networks for Developing Regions and Measuring Internet Performance. These courses interested me a lot as they taught me more about securing yourself from threats but also a lot of practical experience with tools and methods that I find myself using on a weekly basis. All the networking courses have sparked my interest in Cyber Security which I spend time on as much as I can.

Another interesting course was Computer Graphics and 3D printing, where in C++ we wrote a program which allowed us to create Constructive Solid Geometry objects, which we later printed with the Departments 3D printer. This course was very difficult but also very rewarding as we ended up using our own program to make a personal model and printed it.

Lastly, I took a course in User Experience in Games, which involved taking my 3rd year Terminal Reality project and changing it into a pure Horror game to see how it would affect the users. This course was very enjoyable as I loved the Games Development part of it and testing with users. The report I wrote for this module achieved the top mark in the class.
